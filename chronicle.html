Chapter One The City of Nine Gates 九門之城
Peking · 3–17 September 1923

A fortnight of preparation, all of it before departure. Four people are assembled for a scientific expedition west — and discover, reading between the lines of a 31-page briefing, that their American sponsor has appended three sites nobody will describe, one of them currently occupied. Peking smells of coal smoke and the sour pomegranate tree; a telegram reports 140,000 feared dead under the Kantō earthquake; the summer heat has just broken into thin September gold.

I.

The Courtyard off Dongsi

Chih-yüan and Hélène read the briefing at the study table — "Preliminary Research Expedition to the Caves of the Thousand Buddhas… September–December 1923," sealed by the university and the Geological Survey, with Wang Enlai's inked note introducing the American, Warner. Hélène's eye catches three grid references — W-7, W-8, W-11 — clustered, unlabeled, footnoted only "to be distributed at briefing." Both fail their History rolls: Pelliot's 1908 looting is not yet known to them. "Ils ont délibérément omis les descriptions."

II.

Dawn Prayers, and a Scaling Error

After nitnem, Gurdit works over his security assessment — Personnel, Route, Equipment, Timing, and five open questions. His predecessor, the British ex-pat Fenton, fell to dysentery in late August; Gurdit has had the briefing eight days. Studying the reduced Stein map, he catches what Fenton missed.

Navigate 60 — Extreme Success. The Anxi-to-Mogao final leg is compressed on the map: it is roughly 1.5 days longer with laden camels than shown. In Punjabi, to no one: "Fenton should have seen this."

In sceneGurditFenton
III.

The Kit That Cannot Know

At the Peking Union Medical College, Anna Wolf, RN presents Marie's medical kit — three crates, thirty-five pounds, laudanum standing in for the requested morphine. Marie judges it sound.

Medicine roll — a 94, failure of the right kind. She does not know what she does not know: nothing for kala-azar, nothing for "sand lung," no account of the alkaline wells between Anxi and Dunhuang in a dry year — and this has been a dry year.

In sceneMarieAnna Wolf
IV.

The Three O'Clock Meeting

In the Geology Building the four meet Wang Enlai — and each other — for the first time, in Mandarin. Hélène presses on the anomalous sites and Wang admits the truth: W-7, W-8, W-11 are Warner's private interest, off the Survey permits, drawn from a pre-modern manuscript by a Shanghai collector Warner will not name and has not shared. W-11 is "a site of active religious practice, currently occupied, affiliation uncertain." Gurdit walks his list: Fenton's notes, Wei-valley bandits, the unvetted Liu Dequan, the Anxi distance error (Wang stares at the blackboard), firearms. Chih-yüan asks a single question — is the collector still living? Wang does not know.

V.

The Range

The four are alone together for the first time in the corridor; Gurdit shoulders the heaviest crate and proposes the marksmanship assessment now, in the failing light. He hands Marie a .22 Winchester; she checks the chamber without thinking, and he notes it.

Firearms (Rifle) 61 — Regular success. Eight of ten, two of them at fifty metres from bad footing. "Tin cans in Vancouver," he says. Her brother taught her — "He died at Passchendaele. I had two brothers there." Gurdit answers first in Punjabi, then Mandarin. They clean the rifle in a comfortable silence.

In sceneGurditMarie
VI.

The Corner Table

At the hutong restaurant, in French, Hélène performs a mock tragedy — "Notre directeur de la sécurité est absolument magnifique" — the beard, the eyes, the way he holds a room; probably married, deeply devout, entirely impervious to Frenchwomen. Chih-yüan corrects her gently: Gurdit is a widower — wife and two children lost to influenza in 1918. "I do not think Singh-ji speaks of it." She puts her hand over his.

In sceneChih-yüanHélène
VII.

Downtime — What Is Packed, and What Is Not

Over the 4th to the 6th: Fenton's notes prove locked in a personal trunk at the British Legation, out of reach — but his two annotated Survey maps surface (with a half-drunk bottle of Scotch). Examining them, Chih-yüan finds east of Anxi a partially-erased symbol: a closed, roughly circular form with radiating lines — a sun-diagram, or something else — and beneath it three characters that are not Chinese and not any romanization he knows. He traces it exactly, pockets it, and tells no one. He leaves behind his father's calligraphy and the notebook of poems; Marie receives a confidential list of fourteen Presbyterian mission contacts, one of them — Dunhuang — marked only "Changed since last report."

Departure is confirmed for the 17th, barring unforeseen complications.

Threads Left Hanging

  • W-7, W-8, W-11. Three off-permit sites nobody will describe — and W-11 is occupied, by a community of "uncertain affiliation."
  • The secret manuscript. Warner's source: a pre-modern text in a "Central Asian dialect," from a Shanghai collector who may or may not be alive.
  • The erased symbol. Chih-yüan's tracing sits folded in his field journal, unshown — even to Hélène.
  • Fenton's trunk. His real field notes are locked at the British Legation, inaccessible without his authorization.
  • Liu Dequan. The Lanzhou caravan intermediary, recommended by Fenton, references not yet verified.
  • The kit that cannot know. Kala-azar, sand-lung, alkaline wells — gaps Marie failed to see and does not know exist.
Chapter Two Wagons-Lits 臥車
Peking to Dunhuang · 7 September – October 1923

The American is finally in the room. Beneath the stated errand — rescuing murals a garrison of interned White Russians defaced — lies the real one: a phrase those soldiers scrawled again and again on a cave wall, and a matching inscription a thousand years older. On the road west Hélène cracks both. At the end of it waits an old Taoist at the foot of a honeycombed cliff, with a warning of his own: come to me. Not him.

I.

The Wagons-Lits

In the Legation Quarter, on the Rue Marco Polo, Langdon Warner — forty-two, red-haired, a crushing handshake — receives them among Stein's 1907 maps and a red-pencilled Pelliot catalogue. Three chairs for four; he stays standing. "Wang said you'd be worth talking to. Let's find out if he's right." He wants to know what they can actually do in the field. Hélène opens by holding him to account — "four people rearranged their lives on the strength of thirty words and a professor's word of honour." Warner, amused, turns to the room with his eyes on her: "Tell me what you know about the Library Cave."

In sceneWarnerHélèneChih-yüanGurditMarie
II.

The Library Cave

The looting history surfaces — the earlier failed roll now paid in full. Chih-yüan delivers ninety seconds of dates, names and quantities, level and unornamented: Wang Yuanlu's 1900 discovery; Stein's forty thousand manuscripts for £220 in silver; Pelliot reading Sogdian and Sanskrit in situ and shipping the Diamond Sutra to Paris; the Chinese government moving in 1910 to find the best already gone. The facts editorialize themselves. Warner: "Good. Then you understand why time matters."

In sceneChih-yüanHélèneWarner
III.

What He Actually Needs

The stated purpose: stabilize murals endangered by the White Russian soldiers interned at the caves in 1921–22, with a new chemical preservative. Then the real one — "something those soldiers left behind. Not damage. Writing." He produces a photograph: Cyrillic scrawl looping over a twelve-hundred-year-old fresco — and in one cave it is denser, repeated, the same phrase over and over, in what looks like many hands. He needs it translated. Hélène takes the job; the deadline is the fifteenth. "After that we're moving whether the translation is finished or not."

In sceneWarnerHélène
IV.

The Measure of the Others

Gurdit introduces the party, downplaying himself, and slips — says "Fenton" for the ill predecessor; Warner corrects him quietly. Pressed on whether he has done the corridor: "Not this corridor. The Frontier, extensively. Afghanistan. I know how caravans move and I know how men behave when they think no one is watching." Warner accepts him. Marie watches, taking her own quiet accounting. On the 11th, Liu Dequan's telegram lands: camels secured, water amended to Gurdit's figures — and "ONE MATTER FOR DISCUSSION ON ARRIVAL LANZHOU."

In sceneGurditMarieWarnerLiu Dequan
V.

The Road West

Before dawn on the 17th the cars leave Peking, a soldiers' truck ahead, into grey loess country. Gurdit sleeps deeply for the first time in months, soothed by Chih-yüan's stillness as he reads by lamplight; Chih-yüan realizes the Sikh's warmth toward him is genuine, not secondhand. Hélène sits near Gurdit at every stop, asking sharp questions about the Frontier — building something she has not yet decided the shape of. At dawn Marie has the Lee-Enfield broken down on a cloth before he has finished his tea; they shoot forty minutes — his groupings tighter, hers more consistent.

VI.

The Same Sentence, a Thousand Years Apart

In the Kansu Corridor Hélène works two hours, then goes still, and reads it aloud — French first, then English for the car: "They are come. The end is upon us. For they are come." The same sentence, in many hands, over and over. Warner receives it without visible surprise — "That's what I thought it said" — which is itself information. Pressed, he yields a hand-copy of the collector's manuscript: Tocharian, the collector guessed; directions, Warner thinks.

Linguistics 40 — Perfect Success. "This is not Tocharian. It is Sogdian. Late Sogdian — eighth century." And not directions: the same warning as the Russian. "Someone wrote this in the eighth century. Someone else wrote the same thing on the same walls in Russian, one thousand years later." — "You should have led with this." — "Yes. I should have."

VII.

The Caves of the Thousand Buddhas

A hidden valley that has no business being there; bare October trees, a raven on every branch; the honeycombed cliff with its ladders like the rigging of a ship run aground in the Gobi. At the base waits Wang Yuanlu — near seventy-five, Taoist robes, unhurried — who addresses them all: "你们来了很久了。" ("You have been a long time coming.") His eyes measure Warner and move away. To Chih-yüan, who introduces the party without ever naming Warner, the old priest already knows: "你的老师是个好人。" ("Your teacher is a good man.") He leads them past the ravaged murals to their painted quarters, where the eyes on the walls follow — and at the threshold: "来找我。不是他。" ("Come to me. Not him.")

VIII.

Gates That Should Not Be Opened

Hélène recites the phrase in Mandarin — "他们来了。末日降临。因为他们来了。" Wang Yuanlu stills, then asks, with new attention, where she saw it, and leads her and Chih-yüan to an intact Tang chamber and a white bodhisattva of absolute equanimity. "This phrase has existed here much longer than the Russians." Who are they? — "有些门，不应该被打开。" ("There are gates that should not be opened.") … "'They' are those who disagree." Do you stand watch at them? — "我们都是。有些人不知道自己在守护。" ("We all do. Some do not know they are guarding.")

That night the lamp makes the painted musicians seem to move. Chih-yüan lies awake, turning over some do not know they are guarding and the tracing folded in his journal. He still does not take it out. At dawn Marie and Gurdit share tea and rifles on a flat rock; the ravens watch; Warner is already up the ladders with a lantern, red hair against the cliff — and Wang Yuanlu stands at the base, watching him.

The Warning, in Two Hands

Cyrillic, 1921–22 · Sogdian, 8th century — the same sentence

"They are come. The end is upon us. For they are come." 他们来了。末日降临。因为他们来了。 — scrawled by interned soldiers over a Tang fresco, and inscribed a thousand years before them in the margins of three caves.

Threads Left Hanging

  • Who are "they"? An entity or faction whose coming was inscribed across a thousand years, in two languages, on the same walls.
  • What is behind the wall. Warner's real fixation, still unspoken — he believes the repeated phrase marks something.
  • "Not him." Wang Yuanlu has explicitly set himself against Warner and aligned with Chih-yüan. He knows more than he has said.
  • The gates cosmology. Guardians who watch — some unknowingly. The campaign's metaphysical spine, now named aloud.
  • Liu Dequan's "one matter." Still waiting at Lanzhou, behind them now on the road.
  • The tracing. Chih-yüan's erased symbol has still not been shown — and the Sogdian marginalia in three caves may be its cousin.
